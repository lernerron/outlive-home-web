## Problem Statement

Outlive Homes is a pre-revenue aging-in-place home remodeling company preparing to launch in South Florida. The website exists but isn't live. There are two homepage designs (production at `/` and a premium redesign at `/testing`), but no dedicated service pages, no lead qualification, and no partner presence. The site cannot yet receive paid advertising traffic effectively because:

1. The premium homepage design is stranded at `/testing` instead of production
2. There are no deep service pages for the launch product (barrier-free shower conversions)
3. The lead capture form doesn't qualify leads (urgency, service type, relationship)
4. There is no partner page for referral network development
5. Paid traffic from Houze, Yelp, and Facebook needs a focused landing experience

## Demand Evidence

- **Category validation:** Multiple successful companies operate in this exact space, proving demand
- **Founder has talked to people** in the industry who confirmed need and pain points
- **Market data:** 87% of adults 65+ want to age in place; only 10% of U.S. homes are senior-accessible. The $20B Home Accessibility & Safety market grows at 8-9% annually
- **Urgency signal:** Post-fall and post-surgery customers need modifications within days, not months
- **Gap identified:** No trusted brand owns the full experience (design + construction + financing) in a fragmented market

## Status Quo

The market is fragmented across four friction points:
1. **Trust gap** — No recognized brand in the category. Customers don't know who to trust
2. **Coordination burden** — Customers juggle designers, contractors, and equipment vendors separately
3. **Speed mismatch** — Post-incident urgency (days) vs. contractor timelines (weeks/months)
4. **Financing opacity** — Unclear what's covered by insurance, VA benefits, Medicaid waivers

Outlive's thesis: be the first to combine all four into a single branded experience.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Narrowest wedge:** Barrier-free shower conversions for South Florida seniors (and their adult children making decisions on their behalf).

**Archetype profiles:**
- **Post-incident:** 75-year-old who fell in the shower last week. Daughter in another state is now researching options urgently. Finds Outlive via Facebook ad or Yelp.
- **Proactive planner:** 68-year-old who watched a friend go through a fall. Healthy now, researching options. Finds Outlive via Google or Houze lead marketplace.

**Launch focus:** D2C consumer acquisition via paid channels (Houze, Yelp, Facebook). Referral partnerships developed in parallel but secondary to paid leads initially.

## Premises

1. The website's #1 job is converting paid D2C traffic into qualified leads
2. The premium homepage at `/testing` should replace the current production homepage
3. One deep bathroom safety landing page > three shallow service cards
4. Lead qualification fields (service type, urgency, who-it's-for) are essential for prioritizing follow-up
5. A basic partner page with OutliveHome.org cross-link is needed for credibility, even if D2C is the primary channel
6. Launch with bathroom safety only; home access products (elevators, stairlifts, ramps) come later

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Launch-Ready Landing Machine (CHOSEN)
Promote premium homepage to production. Build one deep Bathroom Safety service page optimized for paid traffic. Add lead qualification fields. Add basic partner page with OutliveHome.org cross-link.

**Effort:** M (human: ~2 weeks / CC: ~2-3 hours)

Skip the full site. Build one hyper-focused landing page for "barrier-free shower conversion" that paid ads point directly to.

**Effort:** S (human: ~3 days / CC: ~45 min)
**Risk:** Low — fast to test
**Pros:** Fastest path to running ads. Focused conversion.
**Cons:** No brand site. Partners see a thin site. Doesn't leverage existing work.

### Deliverable 2: Bathroom Safety Landing Page (`/services/bathroom-safety`)

New dedicated service page optimized for paid traffic conversion. Section order optimized for conversion (objection-handling before form):

**Background color sequence:** Hero (anchor/dark) → Trust Bar (anchor/dark, continuous) → Problem (white) → Solutions (bg-gray) → Process (white) → Before/After (bg-gray) → FAQ (white) → Lead Form (anchor/dark).

1. **Hero** — Problem-first: "A Safer Shower. Installed This Week." Dark background (`bg-anchor` with gradient overlay), terracotta CTA, phone number visible as `tel:` link. All CTA buttons open LeadCaptureForm modal (matching HomePremium pattern). Hero CTA pre-selects "Barrier-free shower" in the service type dropdown.
2. **Trust bar** — Licensed & Insured, CAPS Certified, Satisfaction Guaranteed, Financing Available (verifiable credentials only — no volume claims until real numbers exist). NOTE: Wireframe shows "500+ Bathrooms Completed" — REMOVE this, it's a false claim pre-launch.
3. **Problem section** — Stats (235K ER visits, 80% bathroom falls), empathy-first copy. 2-column layout: narrative left, stat grid right.
4. **Three productized solutions** — Barrier-free shower, Roll-in shower, Complete accessible bathroom. Roll-in shower (middle tier) gets a "Most Popular" badge (`bg-warm text-white text-xs font-semibold px-3 py-1 rounded-full`). Pricing pulled from `data/services.json` config file. Schema: `[{ id: string, name: string, startingPrice: number | null, description: string, timeline: string, features: string[] }]`. When `startingPrice` is null, render "Get a Free Quote" instead of a price. Each card renders 3-4 features from the `features` array as a checklist. If ALL prices are null, add section note: "All pricing is determined during your free in-home assessment." If a service has 0 features, do not render the features section of that card.
5. **Process** — 3-step: Free Assessment → Design & Fixed Quote → Professional Installation (3-5 days). Reuse timeline pattern from HomePremium.
6. **Before/after** — Visual transformation. Use Unsplash stock images at launch (search: "accessible bathroom modern", "barrier-free shower"). Desktop: side-by-side with arrow separator. Mobile: vertical stack, "Before" above "After", downward arrow. 1-2 pairs at launch. If no suitable images sourced by implementation time, show single bathroom hero image under "Our Work" heading without before/after comparison.
7. **FAQ** — Top 5 objections (timeline, financing, aesthetics, what-if-unsure, licensing). Use shadcn/ui `<Accordion type="single" collapsible>` with `defaultValue="item-0"` (first FAQ open on load). Chevron icon rotates 180° on open. Open item gets `border-l-2 border-warm` accent. Keyboard nav and ARIA attributes handled natively by shadcn/ui.
8. **Qualified lead form** — Enhanced with service type, urgency, and relationship fields. Inline form on dark (`bg-anchor`) background using DESIGN.md dark form pattern. This serves as the scroll-past catch-all; hero CTAs use the modal form. Testimonial section placeholder: when real testimonials exist, insert between Process and Before/After.

Visual wireframe archived at `docs/wireframes/bathroom-safety-v1.html`. Layout: 3-column grid for solutions, 2-column hero, single-column mobile, before/after side-by-side with arrow separator (vertical on mobile).

### Deliverable 3: Enhanced Lead Form with Qualification

**Form state specifications (per DESIGN.md):**
- **Error state:** `border-warm text-warm` (NOT red). Error messages: `text-sm text-warm mt-1`.
- **Success state:** `bg-anchor/10 border-anchor text-anchor` (NOT green). Success icon uses anchor color.
- **Submitting state:** Submit button shows spinner icon, button disabled, `opacity-70`. Text changes to "Submitting..."

- **Inline form success (InlineLeadForm):** Show "Thank you! We'll be in touch within 24 hours." with a "Submit Another Request" link that resets the form. Auto-dismiss after 10 seconds.
- Fix all existing brand token violations in both forms during this deliverable (replace `bg-red-*`, `text-red-*`, `bg-green-*`, `text-green-*`, `bg-blue-600` with brand tokens).

**API changes:** The existing `/api/leads` route forwards the request body as JSON to the Google Sheets webhook. New fields should pass through automatically if the webhook/Apps Script accepts arbitrary JSON keys. Implementation steps:
- Add `serviceType`, `urgency`, and `relationship` fields to form state and validation
- Include these fields in the POST body to `/api/leads`
- Update Google Sheets webhook to accept new columns: `serviceType`, `urgency`, `relationship`, `leadType`, `organization`, `role`. Test webhook with curl before building any new forms.
- All three fields are optional — the form should still submit without them (backward compatible)
- Field names: `serviceType` (string), `urgency` (string), `relationship` (string)

These fields enable lead scoring: an "urgent + myself + barrier-free shower" lead gets called first.

### Responsive Strategy

The target audience browses on phones (adult children researching) and tablets (seniors). Key mobile requirements:
- All layouts collapse to single-column below 768px
- Phone number in hero must be a `tel:` link (tap-to-call) on mobile
- Minimum touch targets: 48px (WCAG 2.5.5, critical for aging users)
- Sticky CTA bar on mobile (existing pattern from Layout.jsx) with phone icon + form button
- Product solution cards stack vertically on mobile. "Most Popular" badge remains visible at top of middle card.
- FAQ uses shadcn/ui Accordion (one open at a time) to conserve scroll depth. Keyboard accessible.
- Before/after: stacks vertically on mobile (Before above After, downward arrow separator instead of horizontal arrow)
- Partner form: Message field optional, reduced textarea height on mobile (2 rows instead of 4)

## The Assignment

**Before you build more website, find one real person.** Not a segment, not an archetype — a specific human. Go to a local senior center, a hospital discharge planning office, or an OT practice in South Florida. Ask them: "When one of your clients needs their bathroom modified after a fall, what do you tell them to do?" Listen to the answer. That conversation will tell you more about how to write your landing page than any amount of design iteration.

You can build the site in parallel — but that one conversation should happen this week.
